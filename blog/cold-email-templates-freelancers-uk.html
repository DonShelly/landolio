The 3‑Line Personalisation Framework

Every email below follows this structure:

  1. Personal hook (prove you’re not spamming)
  2. Specific problem you can solve
  3. Simple next step (15‑minute call or reply)

Template 1: The Referral Warm‑Cold Email

Subject: [Name] suggested I reach out

Hi [Name],

[Referrer] mentioned you’re working on [project]. I help [type of client] with [specific outcome].

If you’re open to it, I’d love to share 2–3 quick ideas I noticed on [site/product]. No pitch — just useful feedback.

Would a 15‑minute call be helpful next week?

Template 2: The Quick Win Audit

Subject: 2 quick improvements for [Company]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific issue] on [page/flow]. Two quick wins that usually improve [metric]:
1) [Suggestion 1]
2) [Suggestion 2]

If you want, I can send a 1‑page audit with exact fixes. Would that be useful?

Template 3: The Outcome‑First Pitch

Subject: Idea to increase [metric] at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I help [industry] teams achieve [specific outcome]. Recently I helped [similar client] improve [metric] by [result].

If [Company] is working on [goal], I’d be happy to share what worked. Interested?

Template 4: The “Not a Fit?” Break‑Up Email

Subject: Should I close this out?

Hi [Name],

I haven’t heard back, so I don’t want to be a pest. Should I close the loop on this?

Template 5: The Hiring‑Trigger Email

Subject: Saw the [role] opening at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I noticed you’re hiring a [role]. If the workload is urgent, I can help as a freelancer in the interim — [specific skill] with availability this month.

Worth a quick chat?

Template 6: The Partnership Angle

Subject: Partnership idea for [Company]

Hi [Name],

I work with [type of clients] who often need [service you offer]. If you’re open to referrals, I’d love to explore a simple partnership where we send each other warm leads.

Template 7: The Follow‑Up (Polite Nudge)

Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name], just bumping this in case it got buried. Happy to send the audit or share examples if helpful.

Cold Email Mistakes That Kill Replies

  • ❌ Writing paragraphs (keep it short)
  • ❌ Talking about yourself, not their problem
  • ❌ Asking for a 60‑minute call (aim for 10–15)
  • ❌ No proof you researched them
