Updated: 12 March 2026 · Tested and compared

These tools make the process faster, more professional, and more effective. I've split them into free tools you can use right now and paid solutions for when the problem is serious or ongoing.

Free tools

1. Payment Reminder Email Generator Free

What it does: Generates professional payment reminder emails based on your invoice details. Choose from friendly, firm, or final notice tone — it produces the exact email text you can copy and send.

Best for: When you need to send a chase email right now and don't want to spend 20 minutes agonising over the wording.

How it works: Enter the client name, invoice number, amount, and how overdue it is. Select your preferred tone. Get a professionally worded email in seconds.

Verdict: The fastest way to send a professional chase email. No signup, no account, no learning curve.

2. Late Payment Interest Calculator Free

What it does: Calculates exactly how much statutory interest and fixed compensation your client owes under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

Best for: Adding teeth to your chase emails. When a client sees "You owe £500 plus £47.82 in statutory interest and £40 compensation," they take it seriously.

Key feature: Uses the current Bank of England base rate automatically. Shows the daily interest accrual so the client knows it's growing.

Verdict: Essential knowledge for any freelancer chasing a late payment. Most people don't realise they have these legal rights.

→ Try the Interest Calculator (free)

3. Should I Chase This Invoice? Free

What it does: A decision tool that analyses your specific situation — invoice amount, days overdue, client relationship, chase history — and gives you a clear action plan with urgency scoring.

Best for: When you're not sure whether to escalate, wait, or write it off. Removes the emotional guesswork.

Verdict: Surprisingly useful when you're going back and forth about what to do. Gives you objective guidance based on UK law and best practice.

6. Chaser From £50/month

What it does: Automated invoice chasing software. Connects to your accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage) and automatically sends payment reminders on a schedule you set.

Best for: Businesses sending 20+ invoices per month who need automated, hands-off chasing. Overkill for most solo freelancers.

Downside: Monthly subscription adds up. At £50/month, you need to be recovering significantly more than that in late payments to justify the cost.

Verdict: Good for agencies and busy businesses. Most solo freelancers will get better ROI from templates + manual follow-up.

7. GoCardless 1-2% per transaction

What it does: Direct Debit collection. Instead of sending invoices and waiting, you set up Direct Debit with clients and collect payment automatically on the due date.

Best for: Retainer clients and recurring work. Eliminates late payments entirely by collecting money before it becomes overdue.

Downside: Requires client cooperation to set up the Direct Debit mandate. Some clients resist. Transaction fees eat into margins on small invoices.

Verdict: The best prevention is automatic collection. If you have retainer clients, this eliminates the problem at source. For project-based work, not always practical.
