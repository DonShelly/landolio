Final Demand Letter for Unpaid Invoice (UK): Template + Escalation Timeline

Published: 2026-03-04 · For UK freelancers and small businesses

If a client has ignored multiple reminders, sending another “friendly nudge” usually won’t fix it. At that stage, you need a clear final demand that sets out amount due, deadline, and what happens next.

This guide gives you a practical template you can customise and send today.

When to send a final demand

Typical flow:

  • Invoice issued with clear payment terms
  • Friendly reminder shortly after due date
  • Firm reminder with payment options
  • Final demand letter (this step)

Don’t send final demand too early. It should signal that informal chasing is over.

What to include

  • Your business details and client details
  • Invoice number(s), amount, and original due date
  • Any contractual/statutory interest where applicable
  • A specific payment deadline (e.g. 14 days from date of letter)
  • Next step if unpaid (formal legal route)

Copy-and-paste final demand template

Subject: Final Demand — Invoice [INV-XXXX] (£[amount]) Dear [Client Name], I am writing regarding Invoice [INV-XXXX], issued on [date], with payment terms of [X] days. The invoice is now overdue and remains unpaid. Outstanding balance: £[amount] Original due date: [date] Please arrange payment in full by [date — usually 7 to 14 days from today]. If payment is not received by this deadline, I will proceed to formal recovery action without further notice. This may include a claim for the principal debt, applicable interest, and recovery costs. If there is a genuine dispute, please provide full written details by return so we can resolve this promptly. Payment details: [bank details / payment link] Regards, [Your name] [Business name] [Contact details]

Common mistakes that weaken your position

  • Vague deadlines (“ASAP” instead of a date)
  • No clear amount due
  • Threats you don’t actually follow through on
  • Emotion-heavy wording instead of factual wording
Important: Keep your letter factual and professional. If your case goes further, your communication trail becomes evidence.

If they still ignore you

Once the deadline passes, move to the next formal step. Repeating informal reminders can accidentally train late payers to keep delaying.
