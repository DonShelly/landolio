What Must a UK Invoice Include?

Under UK law (and HMRC guidance), a valid invoice should contain:

  • Your business name and contact details
  • Client's name and address
  • Unique invoice number — sequential, no gaps
  • Invoice date and due date
  • Description of goods or services provided
  • Amount due — including VAT if registered
  • VAT number (if VAT registered)
  • Payment terms and bank details

Do Self-Employed People Need to Send Invoices?

Strictly speaking, self-employed sole traders aren't legally required to issue invoices to non-VAT-registered customers. However, invoices are essential for record-keeping and are required if you're VAT registered. Even if you're not, professional invoices help you:

  • Get paid faster (clients know exactly what to pay)
  • Keep clean records for your Self Assessment tax return
  • Look professional and build trust
  • Prove income if applying for mortgages or loans
