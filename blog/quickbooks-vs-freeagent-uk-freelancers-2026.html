QuickBooks vs FreeAgent for UK Freelancers (2026): Which Should You Choose?

Updated: 12 March 2026 · Comparison for UK self-employed

Choosing between QuickBooks and FreeAgent is one of the most common decisions UK freelancers face. Both are solid accounting tools, both are MTD-compatible, and both will handle your invoices and expenses. But they're built for different people.

This comparison is written specifically for UK freelancers, sole traders, and contractors — not US businesses, not large companies. Here's how they actually compare in 2026.

FreeAgent: built for UK freelancers

✅ Pros

  • File self-assessment directly to HMRC
  • Purpose-built for UK sole traders
  • Free with NatWest, RBS, or Mettle bank accounts
  • Built-in CIS, payroll, and corporation tax
  • Simpler for people who aren't accountants
  • Automatic mileage tracking
  • Excellent UK-specific tax forecasting

❌ Cons

  • £19/month is expensive if you don't get it free via a bank
  • Fewer third-party integrations than QuickBooks
  • Interface can feel dated in places
  • Less suitable if you plan to scale significantly
  • Reporting is good but not as customisable

QuickBooks: the global powerhouse

✅ Pros

  • Cheaper entry price (from £6/month)
  • 750+ integrations (Stripe, PayPal, Shopify, etc.)
  • More customisable invoices and reports
  • Better for growing businesses
  • Frequent introductory discounts (often 90% off)
  • Strong mobile app
  • Better suited for multi-currency work

❌ Cons

  • Cannot file self-assessment directly — manual export needed
  • US-centric design adapted for UK (occasional rough edges)
  • Steeper learning curve if you're not familiar with accounting
  • CIS only on higher-tier plans
  • Introductory pricing increases substantially after promo period
  • Payroll is an expensive add-on

Which should you choose?

Choose FreeAgent if:

  • You're a sole trader or freelancer (not a limited company with complex needs)
  • You want to file self-assessment from your accounting software
  • You have a NatWest, RBS, or Mettle bank account (free access)
  • You work in construction (CIS)
  • You want the simplest possible setup with the least accounting knowledge
  • You need built-in payroll at no extra cost

Choose QuickBooks if:

  • Budget is tight and you want the cheapest option
  • You need to integrate with many other tools (Stripe, Shopify, etc.)
  • You plan to grow and potentially hire
  • You work with international clients and need robust multi-currency
  • You're comfortable with a slightly more complex interface
  • Your accountant prefers QuickBooks (many do)

What about Xero?

Xero is the third major option. It sits between QuickBooks and FreeAgent in complexity and is particularly popular with accountants. See our Xero vs FreeAgent comparison for details.

MTD compliance in 2026

From April 2026, self-employed people earning over £50,000 must keep digital records and submit quarterly updates under Making Tax Digital. Both QuickBooks and FreeAgent are HMRC-recognised MTD-compatible software.

If MTD applies to you, either tool will handle the quarterly submissions. FreeAgent's UK-first approach means MTD features tend to be released earlier and more smoothly.

Do you even need accounting software?

If you're just starting out with simple income and expenses, a well-organised spreadsheet can work for your first year. See our spreadsheet vs software comparison and our free expense tracker tool.

However, once you cross about £20,000 in annual turnover or have more than a handful of clients, dedicated software pays for itself in time savings.
