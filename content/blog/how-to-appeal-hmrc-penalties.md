# How to Appeal HMRC Penalties: Step-by-Step Guide for 2026

**The 31 January Self Assessment deadline has passed.** If you filed late or missed a payment, HMRC penalties are already accumulating. But here's what most people don't know: **many penalties can be appealed successfully** if you act fast and follow the right process.

## Understanding HMRC Penalties (2026 Rules)

First, let's clarify what penalties you're facing. HMRC applies different penalties depending on what you missed:

### Self Assessment Penalties

| Missed Deadline | Penalty | When It Applies |
|----------------|---------|-----------------|
| Online filing (31 Jan) | £100 fixed | Immediate (from 1 Feb 2026) |
| 3 months late | £10/day (max £900) | Starts 1 May 2026 |
| 6 months late | £300 or 5% of tax (whichever is higher) | Starts 1 Aug 2026 |
| 12 months late | £300 or 5% of tax (whichever is higher) | Starts 1 Feb 2027 |
| Payment late (31 Jan) | 5% of unpaid tax | Immediate |
| 6 months late | Additional 5% of unpaid tax | Starts 1 Aug 2026 |
| 12 months late | Additional 5% of unpaid tax | Starts 1 Feb 2027 |

**Interest** also applies to unpaid tax at HMRC's official rate (currently 7.75% annually).

### VAT Penalties

| Offence | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| Late VAT return | £100 minimum (varies by turnover) |
| Repeated late returns | Surcharge escalates (2%→5%→10%→15%) |
| Late VAT payment | Penalty + interest on unpaid amount |

### Corporation Tax Penalties

| Missed Deadline | Penalty |
|----------------|---------|
| Filing late | £100 (under 3 months), escalates to £1,000+ |
| Payment late | Interest on unpaid amount |

**Key insight:** Filing penalties and payment penalties are separate. You can file on time but still get payment penalties if you can't pay. Or vice versa.

## Can You Appeal? (The Honest Answer)

Not every penalty is appealable. HMRC's position is clear: **you can only appeal if you have a "reasonable excuse"** for missing the deadline.

### What Counts as a Reasonable Excuse?

HMRC accepts 4 main categories:

#### 1. **Unexpected Events**
- Serious illness (you or close family member)
- Bereavement
- Fire, flood, or theft
- Postal delays (must prove you posted in time)
- System failures (HMRC's own website down)

#### 2. **Service Provider Failures**
- Accountant error (if you gave them all info on time)
- Software glitch (must be documented)
- Tax agent went out of business

#### 3. **Financial Hardship**
- Unable to pay due to unforeseen circumstances
- Cash flow crisis from late customer payments
- Business failure beyond your control

**Important:** "I forgot" or "I didn't know the deadline" are NOT reasonable excuses. HMRC expects you to know your filing dates.

#### 4. **First-Time Filers**
If this is your first ever Self Assessment return, HMRC may be more lenient — but you still need to explain why you were late.

### When You CANNOT Appeal

These situations will NOT be accepted:

❌ "I was busy with work"
❌ "I didn't know I needed to file"
❌ "I couldn't afford to pay" (without evidence of genuine hardship)
❌ "My accountant is expensive" (you chose not to use one)
❌ "I thought my employer was paying my tax" (if you're self-employed)

**If you have no reasonable excuse, don't waste time appealing.** Instead, focus on:
- Paying what you owe as quickly as possible
- Setting up a payment plan to stop further penalties
- Getting next year's return filed on time

## How to Appeal HMRC Penalties (Step-by-Step)

If you DO have a reasonable excuse, here's the exact process:

### Step 1: Gather Your Evidence (Do This First)

Before you write anything to HMRC, collect proof of your reasonable excuse:

- **Medical issues:** GP letter, hospital appointment letter, prescription records
- **Bereavement:** Death certificate, funeral details
- **System failures:** Screenshots, error messages, HMRC's own service status reports
- **Accountant error:** Timeline of when you provided information, their response
- **Financial hardship:** Bank statements, evidence of late customer payments, redundancy notice

**Without evidence, your appeal will likely fail.** HMRC needs documentation, not just your word.

### Step 2: File Your Return (If You Haven't Already)

You MUST file your return before appealing the late filing penalty. HMRC will reject appeals if the return is still outstanding.

If you can't pay the tax due, file anyway. You can appeal payment penalties separately and request a payment plan.

### Step 3: Write Your Appeal Letter

Your appeal needs to:

1. State which penalty you're appealing
2. Explain your reasonable excuse (be specific)
3. Attach supporting evidence
4. Confirm you've now filed/paid (or have a plan to pay)

**Reasonable Excuse:**
I was unable to file my return by 31 January 2026 due to [illness/bereavement]. [Specific details: e.g., "I was hospitalised from 15 January to 5 February 2026 following emergency surgery." OR "My father passed away on 20 January 2026 and I was the sole executor of his estate."]

I have attached [medical evidence/death certificate] to support this appeal.

**Actions Taken:**
I filed my return on [date] as soon as I was able. I have now paid the full tax owed / set up a payment plan to clear the outstanding balance.

I request that HMRC cancels the late filing penalty on the grounds of reasonable excuse.

Yours faithfully,

[Your signature]
[Your name]

### Step 4: Send Your Appeal

**Deadline:** You have **30 days from the penalty notice date** to appeal.

**How to send:**

1. **Online (fastest):** Use the "Appeal a penalty for Self Assessment" service at gov.uk
2. **Post:** Send to the address on your penalty notice
3. **Phone:** Call HMRC Self Assessment helpline (0300 200 3310) — they may accept verbal appeals for simple cases

**Pro tip:** Send by recorded delivery if posting. You'll need proof you appealed within 30 days.

### Step 5: Wait for HMRC's Response

HMRC aims to respond within 15 working days, but it can take up to 8 weeks.

**Possible outcomes:**

1. **Appeal accepted** → Penalty cancelled, you receive confirmation
2. **Appeal rejected** → You can request a review or go to tribunal (see below)
3. **More information requested** → Provide additional evidence within the deadline given

## If Your Appeal is Rejected

If HMRC rejects your appeal, you have two options:

### Option 1: Request a Review

You can ask HMRC to review their decision. A different officer will reassess your case.

**Deadline:** 30 days from the rejection letter.

**How:** Write to the address on the rejection letter explaining why you disagree. Provide any new evidence.

**Success rate:** Reviews overturn ~20-30% of initial rejections (source: HMRC statistics).

### Option 2: Appeal to the Tax Tribunal

If HMRC still rejects your appeal, you can take your case to an independent First-tier Tribunal.

**When to do this:**
- You have strong evidence HMRC ignored
- The penalty is large (£500+)
- You're prepared to invest time in the process

**How:**
1. Download form "SSCS1" from gov.uk
2. Complete it with your grounds for appeal
3. Submit within 30 days of HMRC's final rejection

**Cost:** Free for penalties under £1,000. For larger amounts, there may be a fee.

**Success rate:** Tribunals rule in taxpayers' favour ~40% of the time (source: Tribunal Service data).

**Warning:** If you lose at tribunal, you may have to pay HMRC's legal costs (rare for Self Assessment cases, more common for VAT/Corporation Tax disputes).

## Alternative: Payment Plans (If You Can't Pay)

If you can't afford to pay your tax bill, **don't ignore it.** HMRC offers Time to Pay arrangements that let you spread payments over up to 12 months.

### How to Set Up a Payment Plan

**If you owe less than £30,000:**

1. Go to gov.uk and search "Self Assessment payment plan"
2. Log in with your Government Gateway credentials
3. Set up a monthly payment plan online (instant approval)

**If you owe more than £30,000:**

Call HMRC's Payment Support Service: **0300 200 3835**

You'll need to explain:
- Why you can't pay in full
- How much you can afford monthly
- When you'll clear the balance

**What HMRC will agree to:**

- Monthly payments over 3-12 months
- Interest still applies (7.75% annually)
- Late payment penalties may be reduced if you stick to the plan

**Important:** Set up a payment plan BEFORE the first payment deadline. If you wait until after penalties apply, you'll still owe them.

## Preventing Future Penalties

Once you've dealt with this year's penalties, here's how to avoid them next time:

### 1. **Set Calendar Reminders**

Key dates for 2026-27:

| Date | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| 5 April 2026 | Tax year ends |
| 5 October 2026 | Register for Self Assessment (if new) |
| 31 October 2026 | Paper return deadline |
| 31 January 2027 | Online filing + payment deadline |

Set reminders for 2 weeks before each date.

### 2. **File Early**

Don't wait until January. File in the summer when you have time. HMRC's systems are faster, and if there's an issue, you have months to fix it.

### 3. **Save for Your Tax Bill**

If you're self-employed, put aside 25-30% of your income each month into a separate savings account. When the tax bill comes, you'll have the cash ready.

### 4. **Use Accounting Software**

Tools like QuickBooks, Xero, or FreeAgent automatically calculate your tax liability and remind you of deadlines.

**Free option:** HMRC's own Basic Tools (search "HMRC free software" on gov.uk).

### 5. **Get Professional Help**

If your tax affairs are complex (multiple income streams, rental property, capital gains), hire an accountant. The cost is tax-deductible and could save you far more than their fee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do I have to appeal an HMRC penalty?**

30 days from the date on the penalty notice. After that, HMRC may refuse to consider your appeal.

**Can I appeal a penalty if I've already paid it?**

Yes. Paying the penalty doesn't stop you appealing. If your appeal succeeds, HMRC will refund the penalty amount.

**What happens if I don't appeal or pay?**

HMRC can:
- Take the amount from your wages via PAYE
- Freeze your bank account and take the money directly
- Send debt collectors
- Take you to court

Don't ignore penalties. Even if you can't afford to pay, contact HMRC to arrange a payment plan.

**Does HMRC ever cancel penalties automatically?**

Rarely. If there was a system-wide failure (e.g., their website crashed on deadline day), HMRC may cancel penalties for everyone affected. But you usually need to appeal.

**Can I appeal daily £10 penalties?**

Yes. If you have a reasonable excuse for being 3 months late, you can appeal the £10/day penalties too. But you must appeal the £100 initial penalty first.

**What if my accountant made a mistake?**

If you gave your accountant all necessary information in time and they failed to file, that's a reasonable excuse. You'll need evidence (emails, contract, their admission of error).

**Will appealing affect my credit score?**

No. HMRC penalties don't appear on your credit file unless they escalate to County Court Judgments (CCJs), which only happens if you ignore multiple demands.
