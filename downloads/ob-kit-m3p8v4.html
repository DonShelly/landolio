Client Questionnaire & Brief

Send this to every new client before you start work. The answers become the foundation of your scope, timeline, and pricing. Better questions = fewer surprises.

💡 Pro tip

Send this as a Google Form or Notion page so clients can fill it in at their own pace. Include a deadline — "Please complete by [date] so we can start on schedule."

Welcome Email Template

Send this after the client signs the contract and pays the deposit. It sets the tone, confirms the details, and tells them exactly what to expect next.

To: [Client Name] <[client@email.com]> From: [Your Name] <[your@email.com]> Subject: Welcome aboard, [Client Name]! Let's get started 🚀

Hi [Client First Name],

Delighted to officially kick off our project together! I'm really excited about [brief project description] and I'm confident we're going to produce something great.

Here's a quick recap of what we've agreed:

  • Project: [Project name / description]
  • Deliverables: [Key deliverables]
  • Timeline: [Start date] → [End date]
  • Total fee: [£X,XXX]
  • Payment schedule: [e.g. 50% deposit paid, 50% on completion]

What happens next:

  1. Client brief — I've attached the project questionnaire. Please fill this in by [date] so I have everything I need to get started.
  2. Kick-off call — I've suggested [date/time] for a 30-minute call to walk through the brief and answer any questions. Does that work for you?
  3. First update — You can expect the first progress update by [date].

Communication: I'll be sending updates via [email / Slack / etc.]. If you need me urgently, the best way to reach me is [preferred contact method]. My working hours are [e.g. Mon–Fri, 9am–5pm GMT].

If anything's unclear or you have questions at any stage, just ask — that's what I'm here for.

Looking forward to working with you!

[Your Name]

[Your Business Name]
[your@email.com] · [phone number]
[website URL]

💡 Pro tip

Attach the Client Questionnaire (Template 01) and Scope of Work (Template 05) to this email. Send the Payment Terms (Template 04) with the contract/invoice instead.

Project Timeline Template

Share this with the client after the kick-off call. It gives both sides clear expectations on what's happening when — and builds in buffer for feedback rounds.

Project

[Project Name]

Client

[Client Name]

Duration

[X weeks]

Phase Timing Activities Deliverable Owner
Discovery [Week 1] Kick-off call · Review client brief · Research · Gather assets and content Completed brief, asset list Both
🔑 Milestone: Brief signed off by client Client
Create [Week 2–3] Initial concepts · First draft / prototype · Internal review V1 draft for review Freelancer
🔑 Milestone: V1 delivered for client feedback Freelancer
Refine [Week 3–4] Client feedback round · Revisions (up to [2] rounds) · Second review Revised draft Both
🔑 Milestone: Final version approved by client Client
Deliver [Week 5] Final polish · Asset packaging · Handoff · Documentation Final files & handoff Freelancer
🔑 Milestone: Project complete · Final invoice issued Freelancer

💡 Pro tip

Build in buffer. If you think something will take 5 days, tell the client 7. Under-promise and over-deliver. Also note that "Client feedback" phases are usually where projects stall — set explicit deadlines for feedback turnaround (e.g. "feedback within 3 working days").

Key assumptions

  • Client provides all required content/assets by [date]
  • Client feedback will be provided within [X] working days of each deliverable
  • Up to [2] rounds of revisions are included in the project fee
  • Additional revision rounds are charged at [£X] per round
  • Delays caused by late feedback will push the completion date by the equivalent number of days

Template 04 of 06

Payment Terms Agreement

Include this in your contract or send alongside your first invoice. Clear payment terms prevent 90% of payment disputes. These include UK statutory interest references.

Payment Terms Agreement

Between [Your Business Name] ("the Supplier") and [Client Business Name] ("the Client")

Date: [Date]

Clause 1 — Project Fee

The total fee for the project described in the Scope of Work is [£X,XXX] (excluding VAT, if applicable). This fee covers all deliverables and up to [2] rounds of revisions as outlined in the agreed scope.

Clause 2 — Deposit

A deposit of [50%] of the total project fee ([£X,XXX]) is required before work commences. Work will not begin until the deposit has been received in full.

Clause 3 — Payment Schedule

Payments are due as follows:

  • [50%] — On signing / before project commencement
  • [25%] — On delivery of [mid-project milestone]
  • [25%] — On project completion and final delivery

Clause 4 — Payment Terms

All invoices are payable within [14] days of the invoice date. Payment should be made by bank transfer to the account details provided on the invoice.

Clause 5 — Late Payment

If payment is not received within the agreed terms, the Supplier reserves the right to:

  • Charge interest at [8%] above the Bank of England base rate per annum on overdue amounts, in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998
  • Claim statutory compensation of £40–£100 depending on the debt size
  • Suspend all work until the outstanding balance is paid in full
  • Withhold delivery of any completed work or files

Clause 6 — Additional Work

Any work beyond the agreed scope (including additional revision rounds beyond the [2] included) will be quoted separately and must be approved by the Client in writing before work begins. Additional revisions are charged at [£X] per round.

Clause 7 — Cancellation

If the Client cancels the project after work has commenced, the deposit is non-refundable. The Client will be invoiced for any additional work completed beyond the deposit amount at the agreed day rate of [£XXX]/day.

Clause 8 — Ownership & Intellectual Property

Full ownership of the final deliverables transfers to the Client only upon receipt of full and final payment. Until payment is received, all work remains the intellectual property of the Supplier.

Business: [Your Business Name]

Date: [Date]

⚠️ Important

This template uses professional language based on UK contract law, but it is not legal advice. We recommend having a solicitor review your complete contract before use — especially if dealing with large projects or new clients.

Scope of Work Template

The single most important document for preventing scope creep. Define what's included, what's excluded, and what happens when things change. Send this before starting any project.

Scope of Work

[Project Name]

Project Overview

Freelancer [Your Name / Business Name]
Client [Client Name / Business Name]
Project [Project Name]
Start Date [Date]
Target Completion [Date]
Total Fee [£X,XXX] (excluding VAT)

Project Description

[Describe the project in 2-3 paragraphs. What is being created? What problem does it solve? What is the desired outcome? Be specific.]

Deliverables — What's Included

The following deliverables are included in the agreed project fee:

# Deliverable Format Due
1 [e.g. Homepage design] [Figma] [Week 2]
2 [e.g. Inner page templates x3] [Figma] [Week 3]
3 [e.g. Responsive development] [HTML/CSS] [Week 4]
4 [Add more rows as needed] [Format] [Week X]

Exclusions — What's NOT Included

The following are not included in the project fee. If needed, they can be quoted as separate work:

  • [e.g. Copywriting / content creation]
  • [e.g. Photography / stock images]
  • [e.g. Hosting setup & maintenance]
  • [e.g. SEO optimisation]
  • [e.g. Ongoing support beyond handoff]
  • [Add more as needed]

Revisions Policy

  • Up to [2] rounds of revisions are included in the project fee
  • A "revision round" is defined as one set of consolidated feedback from the Client, submitted in a single communication
  • Additional rounds are charged at [£XXX] per round
  • Major scope changes (new pages, features, or directions) are not revisions — they require a separate quote
  • Feedback must be provided within [X] working days. Delays beyond this will push the project timeline accordingly

Approval & Sign-Off

  • Each phase requires written approval (email confirmation is sufficient) before moving to the next
  • Once a phase is approved, revisiting that phase counts as a new revision round
  • The authorised approver for this project is: [Client contact name]
  • If no feedback is received within [X] working days of a deliverable, it will be deemed approved

Client Responsibilities

To keep the project on track, the Client agrees to:

  • Provide all required content, assets, and access by the agreed dates
  • Designate a single point of contact with decision-making authority
  • Consolidate feedback from all stakeholders before submitting
  • Respond to deliverables within [X] working days
  • Adhere to the agreed payment schedule

Change Request Process

Any changes to the agreed scope must follow this process:

  1. Client submits a change request in writing (email is fine)
  2. Freelancer assesses the impact on timeline and cost within [2] working days
  3. Client approves the revised quote and timeline in writing
  4. Work on the change begins only after written approval

By signing below, both parties agree to the scope, deliverables, and terms outlined in this document.
