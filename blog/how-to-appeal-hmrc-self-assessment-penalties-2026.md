# How to Appeal HMRC Self-Assessment Penalties in 2026: Complete Guide

The 31 January 2026 Self-Assessment deadline has passed. If you missed it, you're facing penalties — but **you can appeal**.

HMRC issues over 1 million late filing penalties every year. Many are successfully appealed. This guide shows you exactly how to challenge yours.

## Quick Overview: Self-Assessment Penalties You Can Appeal

| Penalty Type | Amount | When It Applies | Appeal Window |
|--------------|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Initial Late Filing** | £100 | Day after deadline (1 Feb 2026) | 30 days from penalty notice |
| **Daily Penalties** | £10/day (max £900) | 3 months late (1 May 2026) | 30 days from penalty notice |
| **6-Month Penalty** | 5% of tax due (min £300) | 6 months late (1 Aug 2026) | 30 days from penalty notice |
| **12-Month Penalty** | 5% of tax due OR up to 100% | 12 months late (1 Feb 2027) | 30 days from penalty notice |
| **Late Payment Penalty** | 5% of unpaid tax | 30 days after payment deadline (1 Apr 2026) | 30 days from penalty notice |

**Key point:** You have **30 days** from receiving a penalty notice to appeal. Don't wait.

## What Are Valid Grounds for Appeal?

HMRC will only cancel a penalty if you have a "reasonable excuse." Here's what counts:

### ✅ Reasonable Excuses HMRC Accepts

1. **Serious illness or bereavement**
   - You or a close family member was seriously ill
   - Hospitalisation or death of immediate family
   - Mental health crisis that prevented you from filing

2. **Fire, flood, or theft**
   - Your tax records were destroyed
   - Computer equipment containing records was stolen
   - Natural disaster prevented access to documents

3. **Postal delays**
   - You filed by post and HMRC received it late due to postal strikes
   - Proof of posting date required (certificate of posting)

4. **Service issues**
   - HMRC's online service was unavailable when you tried to file
   - Technical failures on Government Gateway prevented submission

5. **Unexpected events beyond your control**
   - Urgent hospital admission on deadline day
   - Domestic emergency (boiler explosion, break-in)
   - Power cut or internet outage on filing day

6. **Professional advice failure**
   - Your accountant failed to file despite you providing information on time
   - You followed incorrect HMRC guidance

### ❌ Excuses HMRC Usually Rejects

- "I forgot" or "I didn't know about the deadline"
- "I was too busy with work"
- "I couldn't afford to pay the tax" (applies to payment penalties only)
- "I had personal problems" (unless severe and documented)
- "My accountant was on holiday"
- "I thought someone else was filing for me"

**Reality check:** HMRC rejects about 40% of penalty appeals. You need evidence, not just an explanation.

## The 3-Step Appeal Process

### Step 1: Gather Evidence (Before You Appeal)

Don't appeal without proof. HMRC wants documentation:

**For illness/bereavement:**
- Doctor's letter confirming dates unable to work
- Hospital admission records
- Death certificate + proof of relationship
- Mental health professional letter

**For technical issues:**
- Screenshots of error messages (with timestamps)
- HMRC service status page confirming outage
- Email correspondence with HMRC support

**For postal delays:**
- Certificate of posting from Royal Mail (dated before deadline)
- Tracking number showing delayed delivery

**For professional failure:**
- Contract/engagement letter with accountant
- Proof you provided information on time (emails, receipts)
- Evidence you chased them before deadline

### Step 2: Write Your Appeal Letter

You can appeal online or by post. Post is often better for complex cases because you can attach evidence.

**Appeal online:** HMRC online account > Self-Assessment > Penalties > Appeal this penalty

**Appeal by post:**
```
HMRC Self-Assessment
BX9 1AS
United Kingdom
```

#### What to Include in Your Appeal

1. **Your details:**
   - Full name and address
   - UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference)
   - National Insurance number
   - Penalty reference number (on the notice)

2. **Clear statement:**
   - "I am appealing the £[amount] penalty issued on [date]"
   - "The penalty reference is [reference]"

3. **Your reasonable excuse:**
   - What happened (be specific)
   - When it happened (dates matter)
   - Why it prevented you from meeting the deadline
   - What you did as soon as you were able

4. **Supporting evidence:**
   - List all documents attached
   - Reference them in your explanation

5. **Action taken:**
   - "I submitted my return on [date] as soon as I was able"
   - "I have now put [safeguards] in place to prevent this happening again"

6. **Clear request:**
   - "I request that you cancel this penalty as I had a reasonable excuse"

### Step 3: Wait for HMRC's Decision

**Timeline:**
- **Simple appeals:** 4-6 weeks
- **Complex appeals:** 8-12 weeks
- **Appeals requiring review:** 3-6 months

**While you wait:**
- You **must still pay** any tax owed (appeals don't stop interest accruing)
- You don't have to pay the penalty while appealing
- HMRC will write to you with their decision

**HMRC's possible responses:**
1. **Appeal accepted** – penalty cancelled, no further action
2. **Appeal rejected** – you have 30 days to request a review or appeal to tribunal
3. **Request for more information** – provide it within the deadline they give

## What If HMRC Rejects Your Appeal?

You have two options:

### Option 1: Request an HMRC Review

A different HMRC officer reviews your case. You have **30 days** from the rejection letter to request this.

**How to request:**
- Online: HMRC online account > Penalties > Request review
- By post: Write to the address on the rejection letter

**State:** "I request an independent review of my penalty appeal under Section 49B TMA 1970."

**Provide:** Any new evidence you didn't include originally.

**Timeline:** HMRC has 45 days to complete the review.

### Option 2: Appeal to the Tax Tribunal

If HMRC rejects your appeal or review, you can appeal to an independent tribunal.

**How to appeal:**
- Complete form EX100 (available on GOV.UK)
- Send to: First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
- Fee: £20-£50 depending on case value

**Timeline:** Tribunals typically hear cases within 6-12 months.

**Success rate:** About 60% of tribunal appeals succeed (higher than HMRC's internal review success rate).

## Special Situations

### You Had Multiple Reasonable Excuses

If you had several overlapping issues (e.g., illness AND postal delay), mention all of them. HMRC considers the cumulative impact.

### You're Facing Daily Penalties (£10/day)

Daily penalties start on 1 May 2026 (3 months after the deadline). If you still haven't filed by then, you'll rack up £10/day up to a maximum of £900 (90 days).

**Strategy:**
1. File your return IMMEDIATELY (even if late) to stop daily penalties accruing
2. Then appeal the penalties using the same reasonable excuse process

### You Can't Afford the Penalty

"I can't afford to pay" is **not** a reasonable excuse for late filing penalties.

However:
- You can apply for Time to Pay (spread payments over 12 months)
- If paying would cause genuine hardship, mention this in your appeal (HMRC may offer a payment plan even if they don't cancel the penalty)

### Your Return Was Filed on Time But HMRC Says It Was Late

Check your confirmation email/receipt. If you have proof of timely filing:
1. Contact HMRC immediately: 0300 200 3310
2. Provide your submission reference and timestamp
3. Request they correct their records and cancel the penalty

This usually resolves within 2-3 weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I appeal a penalty if I've already paid it?**

Yes. Pay it to stop interest accruing, then appeal for a refund. If successful, HMRC will refund the penalty (but not the tax itself).

**What if I genuinely forgot the deadline?**

"Forgetting" is not a reasonable excuse. However, if you have evidence of exceptional circumstances that caused you to forget (e.g., bereavement, serious mental health crisis), this may be accepted.

**How long does HMRC keep penalty records?**

Penalties stay on your record. Repeated late filing makes future appeals less likely to succeed.

**Can I appeal daily penalties separately from the initial £100?**

Yes. You receive separate penalty notices for each penalty type. Appeal each one within 30 days of the notice.

**What if my reasonable excuse only covers part of the late period?**

HMRC may reduce (not cancel) the penalty. For example, if you were ill for 2 weeks but didn't file for 2 months, they might cancel the first £100 but not daily penalties.

**Should I use an accountant to appeal?**

For simple appeals (illness, postal delay, service failure), you can do it yourself. For complex cases (accountant failure, business issues, multiple penalties), professional help may improve your chances.

**What if HMRC loses my appeal letter?**

Always get proof of posting (Royal Mail Certificate of Posting). If HMRC claims they didn't receive it, this is your evidence. Online appeals are tracked automatically.

**Can I appeal more than once?**

No. You get one appeal, one review, and one tribunal hearing. Make your first appeal as strong as possible.
