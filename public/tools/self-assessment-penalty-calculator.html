How HMRC Penalties Work

  • £100 immediate penalty — charged from 1 February 2026, even if you owe £0 tax
  • £10/day after 3 months — starts 1 May 2026, up to £900 (90 days)
  • £300 or 5% after 6 months — whichever is greater (1 August 2026)
  • Another £300 or 5% after 12 months — whichever is greater (1 February 2027)
  • Plus interest on unpaid tax — currently around 7.75% per year, charged daily

What Should I Do?

  1. File immediately — even if you can't pay. Filing stops penalties escalating.
  2. Set up a payment plan — HMRC offers "Time to Pay" for up to 12 months if you owe under £30,000.
  3. Appeal if you have grounds — serious illness, bereavement, or disaster may get the £100 penalty waived.
  4. Don't wait — 1 May 2026 is when daily penalties start (£10/day = £300/month).
