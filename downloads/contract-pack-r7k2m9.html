Template 01 of 06

Basic Freelance Services Agreement

Your essential, all-purpose freelance contract. Use this for any project — it covers scope, payment, intellectual property, confidentiality, liability, and termination in a single document.

💡 When to use this

This is your default contract. Use it for one-off projects, short engagements, or whenever you need a straightforward agreement. For ongoing monthly work, use Template 02 (Retainer) instead. For complex multi-phase projects, use Template 03 (Project-Based).

Freelance Services Agreement

This Agreement is entered into on [DATE]

The Freelancer

[FREELANCER NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

The Client

[CLIENT NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

Clause 1

Scope of Services

The Freelancer agrees to provide the following services (the "Services") to the Client:

[Describe the services in detail — e.g. "Design and development of a 5-page responsive website including homepage, about, services, portfolio, and contact pages, using WordPress with a custom theme."]

The Services shall include [NUMBER] rounds of revisions per deliverable. Any work beyond the scope described above shall be subject to a separate written agreement or Change Order (see Clause 8).

Clause 2

Fees and Payment

The Client shall pay the Freelancer a total fee of [AMOUNT] (the "Fee") for the Services, payable as follows:

  • A non-refundable deposit of [AMOUNT / PERCENTAGE] due upon signing this Agreement
  • The remaining balance of [AMOUNT] due within [NUMBER] calendar days of the Freelancer's final invoice

Invoices shall be submitted to [EMAIL]. All fees are exclusive of VAT, which shall be charged where applicable at the prevailing rate.

Payment shall be made by [bank transfer / PayPal / Wise / Stripe] to the account details provided on the Freelancer's invoice.

Clause 3

Late Payment

If any payment is not received by the due date, the Freelancer reserves the right to:

  1. charge interest at the rate of 8% per annum above the Bank of England base rate, in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (as amended);
  2. claim fixed-sum compensation as prescribed by the said Act (£40 for debts up to £999.99; £70 for debts between £1,000 and £9,999.99; £100 for debts of £10,000 or more);
  3. suspend all work until payment is received in full; and
  4. recover all reasonable costs of collection, including solicitor's fees.

Clause 4

Timeline and Delivery

The Freelancer shall use reasonable endeavours to deliver the Services by [DATE or "within [NUMBER] weeks of receiving the deposit and all required materials"]. Delivery timelines are estimates and not binding deadlines unless expressly stated otherwise in writing.

The timeline shall be extended by a reasonable period where delays are caused by the Client, including but not limited to late provision of materials, feedback, content, or approvals.

Clause 5

Intellectual Property

Upon receipt of payment in full, the Freelancer assigns to the Client all intellectual property rights in the final delivered work product (the "Deliverables"), including copyright, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

The following are expressly excluded from this assignment:

  • Any pre-existing materials, tools, frameworks, or code owned by the Freelancer prior to this engagement (the "Freelancer's Background IP"), which are licensed to the Client on a non-exclusive, perpetual basis for use in connection with the Deliverables only
  • Any third-party materials (e.g. stock images, open-source libraries) which remain subject to their respective licences
  • Draft, unused, or rejected concepts, which remain the property of the Freelancer

Until payment is received in full, all rights in the Deliverables remain with the Freelancer.

Clause 6

Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep confidential all information received from the other party that is marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential (the "Confidential Information"). Neither party shall disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing party, except:

  • to professional advisers bound by a duty of confidentiality;
  • as required by law, regulation, or court order; or
  • where the information is already in the public domain through no fault of the receiving party.

This obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of [NUMBER — e.g. 2] years.

Clause 7

Limitation of Liability

The Freelancer's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise, shall not exceed the total Fees paid or payable under this Agreement.

The Freelancer shall not be liable for any indirect, consequential, or special losses, including but not limited to loss of profits, loss of revenue, loss of data, or loss of business opportunity, howsoever arising.

Nothing in this Agreement shall limit or exclude liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, or any other liability that cannot be lawfully limited or excluded.

Clause 8

Change Orders

Any work requested by the Client that falls outside the Scope of Services described in Clause 1 shall constitute a "Change Order." No Change Order shall be commenced until the Freelancer has provided a written estimate and the Client has approved it in writing (email shall suffice). Change Orders shall be billed at the Freelancer's standard rate of [AMOUNT] per hour, or at such other rate as mutually agreed.

Clause 9

Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving [NUMBER — e.g. 14] days' written notice to the other party.

Upon termination by the Client:

  • The Client shall pay the Freelancer for all work completed to date, plus a cancellation fee of [PERCENTAGE — e.g. 25%] of the remaining contract value
  • Any deposit paid shall be non-refundable

Upon termination by the Freelancer, the Freelancer shall refund any prepaid amounts for work not yet completed. Upon full payment, the Client shall receive a licence to use all completed Deliverables as per Clause 5.

Clause 10

Dispute Resolution

The parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation within [NUMBER — e.g. 14] business days. If unresolved, either party may refer the dispute to mediation through the Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution (CEDR). If mediation fails, either party may commence court proceedings. The Freelancer may commence proceedings for unpaid fees without first engaging in negotiation or mediation.

Clause 11

General

Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with any schedules or appendices, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

Amendments. No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

Freelancer Status. The Freelancer is an independent contractor and not an employee, worker, agent, or partner of the Client. Nothing in this Agreement creates a relationship of employment, agency, or partnership.

Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

Signed by the Freelancer

Template 02 of 06

Retainer Agreement

For ongoing client relationships with recurring monthly fees. Covers service hours, rollover policy, scope of recurring work, and termination notice periods.

💡 When to use this

Use this when a client wants ongoing access to your services — e.g. monthly design support, regular content creation, ongoing consulting, or maintenance work. It provides predictable income for you and guaranteed availability for the client.

Retainer Services Agreement

This Agreement is entered into on [DATE]

The Freelancer

[FREELANCER NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

The Client

[CLIENT NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

Clause 1

Retainer Services

The Freelancer agrees to provide the following recurring services (the "Retainer Services") to the Client:

[Describe the ongoing services — e.g. "Monthly website maintenance, content updates, performance monitoring, security patches, and up to 4 hours of design work per month."]

The Retainer Services include up to [NUMBER] hours of work per calendar month (the "Included Hours").

Clause 2

Retainer Fee and Payment

The Client shall pay a recurring retainer fee of [AMOUNT] per calendar month (the "Retainer Fee"), due on or before the [NUMBER — e.g. 1st] day of each month. The first Retainer Fee is due upon signing this Agreement.

The Retainer Fee is payable in advance. The Freelancer shall not be obligated to perform the Retainer Services for any month in which the Retainer Fee has not been received.

All fees are exclusive of VAT, which shall be charged where applicable.

Clause 3

Additional Hours

Work exceeding the Included Hours in any calendar month shall be billed at the Freelancer's standard rate of [AMOUNT] per hour. The Freelancer shall notify the Client before commencing work that would exceed the Included Hours and shall not proceed without written approval (email shall suffice).

Additional hours shall be invoiced at the end of the relevant month and are payable within [NUMBER] calendar days.

Clause 4

Unused Hours

Unused Included Hours shall not roll over to subsequent months unless expressly agreed in writing. The Retainer Fee remains payable in full regardless of whether the Client utilises all Included Hours in any given month.

Clause 5

Response Times and Availability

During the term of this Agreement, the Freelancer shall use reasonable endeavours to respond to Client requests within [NUMBER — e.g. 24] hours during normal business hours (Monday to Friday, 9:00–17:30 GMT/BST, excluding English bank holidays).

The Freelancer is not required to be available outside these hours. Urgent out-of-hours requests may be accommodated at the Freelancer's discretion and may incur a rush fee surcharge of [PERCENTAGE]%.

Clause 6

Term and Termination

This Agreement shall commence on [DATE] and shall continue on a rolling monthly basis until terminated by either party.

Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving [NUMBER — e.g. 30] days' written notice. Upon termination:

  • The Client shall pay for all work completed to date, including any additional hours incurred
  • The current month's Retainer Fee shall be non-refundable
  • The Freelancer shall deliver all completed work within [NUMBER] business days of the termination date

Clause 7

Late Payment

If the Retainer Fee or any additional charges are not received by the due date, the provisions of the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (as amended) shall apply. The Freelancer may suspend the Retainer Services until all outstanding amounts are paid in full.

Clause 8

Intellectual Property, Confidentiality, Liability, and General Terms

The intellectual property, confidentiality, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, and general provisions set out in the Freelancer's standard terms (as attached or previously agreed) shall apply to this Agreement. Where the Freelancer's standard terms conflict with this Agreement, this Agreement shall prevail.

Signed by the Freelancer

Template 03 of 06

Project-Based Contract

For defined projects with multiple phases, milestones, and deliverables. Includes a built-in milestone payment schedule so you get paid as you go — not just at the end.

💡 When to use this

Use this for projects worth more than £1,000 or lasting longer than 2–4 weeks. The milestone structure protects you from doing 80% of the work and never getting paid. Each phase has a deliverable and a payment — no payment, no next phase.

Project-Based Services Agreement

This Agreement is entered into on [DATE]

The Freelancer

[FREELANCER NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

The Client

[CLIENT NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[EMAIL]

Clause 1

Project Description

The Freelancer agrees to deliver the following project (the "Project"):

[Project Name — e.g. "Complete Brand Identity Package for Acme Ltd"]

The detailed scope, specifications, and deliverables for the Project are set out in Schedule A attached to this Agreement.

Clause 2

Milestone Schedule

The Project shall be delivered in the following phases. Each milestone payment is due within [NUMBER] calendar days of the Freelancer's invoice for that milestone.

Phase Deliverable Amount Due
Phase 1 [e.g. Research & Discovery] [AMOUNT] Upon signing
Phase 2 [e.g. Initial Concepts] [AMOUNT] On delivery
Phase 3 [e.g. Revisions & Refinement] [AMOUNT] On delivery
Phase 4 [e.g. Final Delivery & Handoff] [AMOUNT] On delivery

Total Project Fee: [TOTAL AMOUNT]

Clause 3

Work Progression

The Freelancer shall not be obligated to commence work on any subsequent phase until payment for the preceding phase has been received in full.

The Client shall have [NUMBER — e.g. 5] business days from delivery of each milestone to review and request revisions. If no written feedback is received within this period, the milestone shall be deemed approved.

Each milestone includes [NUMBER] rounds of revisions. Additional revisions shall be billed as a Change Order at [AMOUNT] per hour.

Clause 4

Timeline

The estimated project timeline is [NUMBER] weeks from commencement. The project start date shall be the date on which the Freelancer receives both the signed Agreement and the Phase 1 payment.

The timeline shall be extended proportionately where:

  • The Client causes delays in providing materials, content, feedback, or approvals
  • Change Orders are approved that expand the scope
  • Milestone payments are received late

Clause 5

Early Termination

If the Client terminates this Agreement before all milestones are complete:

  1. The Client shall pay for all completed milestones in full
  2. The Client shall pay for any partially completed work on a pro-rata or hourly basis (at [AMOUNT] per hour), whichever is greater
  3. The Client shall pay a cancellation fee of [PERCENTAGE]% of the remaining unpaid milestones
  4. Any Phase 1 deposit shall be non-refundable

Clause 6

Late Payment, IP, Confidentiality & General Terms

The late payment, intellectual property, confidentiality, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, and general provisions shall be as set out in the Freelancer's standard terms (as attached or previously agreed, or as per the Basic Freelance Services Agreement). In the event of any conflict, this Project-Based Agreement shall prevail.

Signed by the Freelancer

Signature

Signed by the Client

Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

A mutual NDA that protects both parties' confidential information. Use this before sharing sensitive business details, proprietary processes, client lists, or trade secrets.

💡 When to use this

Sign this before starting work — ideally before the discovery call or brief. It protects you both. Mutual NDAs (where both sides agree to confidentiality) are standard practice and more professional than one-sided ones.

Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

This Agreement is entered into on [DATE]

Party A (The Freelancer)

[FREELANCER NAME]
[ADDRESS]

Party B (The Client)

[CLIENT NAME]
[ADDRESS]

Clause 1

Purpose

The parties intend to exchange certain confidential and proprietary information for the purpose of:

[Describe the purpose — e.g. "Evaluating and undertaking a potential web development project for Party B's e-commerce platform."]

Clause 2

Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by either party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that:

  1. is marked or designated as "confidential," "proprietary," or similar; or
  2. by its nature or the circumstances of its disclosure, a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to: business plans, financial information, client lists, pricing, technical specifications, designs, source code, trade secrets, marketing strategies, and any information relating to the Purpose described above.

Clause 3

Obligations

The Receiving Party agrees to:

  1. keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;
  2. not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent;
  3. use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described above;
  4. take all reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of the information, using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information; and
  5. limit access to Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, or advisers who need to know it for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those in this Agreement.

Clause 4

Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

  1. is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
  2. was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
  3. is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or
  4. is lawfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

Clause 5

Required Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is compelled by law, regulation, or court order to disclose Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and cooperate with any efforts to obtain protective treatment of the information.

Clause 6

Return of Information

Upon written request or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of Confidential Information in its possession and provide written confirmation of such destruction if requested.

Clause 7

Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of [NUMBER — e.g. 2] years from the date of signing, or until terminated by either party with [NUMBER — e.g. 30] days' written notice. The confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for a further period of [NUMBER — e.g. 2] years.

Clause 8

Remedies

The parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief or specific performance in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

Clause 9

Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

Signed by Party A

Signature

Signed by Party B

Scope Change Amendment

A formal addendum to attach to any existing contract when the scope, budget, or timeline needs to change. This prevents scope creep and creates a paper trail for every change.

💡 When to use this

Use this every time a client says "can we just add…" or "actually, I was thinking…" If it's not in the original scope, it gets a Change Order. No exceptions. This single document will protect more of your income than any other template in this pack.

Amendment to Services Agreement

Change Order No. [NUMBER] · Dated [DATE]

Reference

Original Agreement

This Amendment relates to the Services Agreement dated [ORIGINAL DATE] between [FREELANCER NAME] (the "Freelancer") and [CLIENT NAME] (the "Client") for the project known as [PROJECT NAME] (the "Original Agreement").

Section 1

Description of Changes

The parties agree to the following changes to the scope of the Original Agreement:

Work to be Added:

[Describe the additional work in detail — e.g. "Addition of an e-commerce shop page with product listings for up to 20 items, shopping cart functionality, and Stripe payment integration."]

Work to be Removed (if any):

[Describe any work being removed from the original scope, or write "None"]

Reason for Change:

[Brief explanation — e.g. "Client has decided to launch an online shop alongside the brochure website."]

Section 2

Impact on Fees

Original Fee: [ORIGINAL AMOUNT]

Additional Fee for this Change Order: [ADDITIONAL AMOUNT]

Revised Total Fee: [NEW TOTAL]

The additional fee shall be payable [upon completion / 50% upfront and 50% on delivery / as a new milestone].

Section 3

Impact on Timeline

Original Delivery Date: [ORIGINAL DATE]

Revised Delivery Date: [NEW DATE]

The timeline extension of [NUMBER] working days reflects the additional scope and shall not constitute a breach of the Original Agreement.

Section 4

Agreement

All other terms and conditions of the Original Agreement remain unchanged and in full force and effect. In the event of any conflict between this Amendment and the Original Agreement, this Amendment shall prevail.

This Amendment shall not be effective until signed by both parties.

Template 06 of 06

Kill Fee & Early Termination Agreement

A standalone agreement (or addendum) that protects you when a client cancels a project mid-way. Ensures you get paid for completed work plus compensation for lost income and scheduling disruption.

💡 When to use this

Use this as a standalone document when your main contract doesn't include kill fee provisions, or when you want more detailed termination terms than your standard contract provides. Particularly important for large projects where you've blocked out weeks or months of calendar time.

Kill Fee & Early Termination Agreement

Supplementary to the Services Agreement dated [DATE]

The Freelancer

[FREELANCER NAME]
[EMAIL]

The Client

[CLIENT NAME]
[EMAIL]

Clause 1

Applicability

This Kill Fee Agreement applies to the project described as [PROJECT NAME] with a total contract value of [TOTAL FEE], as set out in the Services Agreement dated [DATE] (the "Main Agreement").

Clause 2

Notice Period

Either party may terminate the Main Agreement by providing [NUMBER — e.g. 14] days' written notice (the "Notice Period"). During the Notice Period, the Freelancer shall continue to perform the Services unless instructed otherwise by the Client.

Clause 3

Client-Initiated Cancellation — Kill Fee Schedule

In the event of termination by the Client prior to completion, the following fees shall be payable:

When Cancelled Work Completed Fee Kill Fee
Before work begins (after signing) Deposit non-refundable Deposit amount only
During Phase 1 (0–25% complete) Pro-rata or hourly, whichever is greater [e.g. 25%] of remaining value
During Phase 2 (25–50% complete) All completed milestones + pro-rata current [e.g. 35%] of remaining value
During Phase 3+ (50%+ complete) All completed milestones + pro-rata current [e.g. 50%] of remaining value

For the avoidance of doubt, "pro-rata" means the proportion of the current phase that has been completed, calculated by the Freelancer in good faith based on time spent and deliverables produced.

Clause 4

Freelancer-Initiated Termination

If the Freelancer terminates the Main Agreement, the Freelancer shall:

  1. refund any prepaid amounts for work not yet completed;
  2. deliver all completed work to the Client within [NUMBER] business days; and
  3. not charge a kill fee.

Clause 5

Ownership of Work Upon Cancellation

Upon payment of all amounts due under this Agreement (including the kill fee), the Client shall receive a licence to use all completed deliverables as per the intellectual property provisions of the Main Agreement.

Incomplete, draft, or rejected work shall remain the property of the Freelancer unless separately purchased by the Client at the Freelancer's standard hourly rate.

Clause 6

Genuine Pre-Estimate of Loss

The kill fees set out in Clause 3 represent a genuine pre-estimate of the Freelancer's losses arising from early termination, including but not limited to: lost income from declined engagements, rescheduling costs, administrative burden, and the difficulty of replacing cancelled work at short notice. These fees are not intended as a penalty and shall be enforceable as liquidated damages under English law.

Signed by the Freelancer

Part 2 — Payment Protection

10 Payment Protection Clauses

Drop these clauses into any contract to protect your income. Each clause is self-contained — copy the ones you need and add them to your agreement. UK variant is primary; US variant included where relevant.

💡 How to use these clauses

1. Copy the clause text into your contract. 2. Replace all [PLACEHOLDER] fields. 3. Choose UK or US variant. 4. Combine multiple clauses — most contracts need 3–6 of these. 5. Renumber clauses sequentially in your final contract.

Payment Clause 01

Standard Net-30 Payment Terms

Payment Terms. The Client ([CLIENT NAME]) shall pay the Freelancer ([FREELANCER NAME]) the total fee of [AMOUNT] (the "Fee") for the services described in this Agreement (the "Services"). The Fee shall become due and payable within [NUMBER] calendar days of the date of the Freelancer's invoice (the "Payment Period"). Invoices shall be submitted by the Freelancer to [EMAIL] upon completion of the Services, or at such other intervals as agreed in writing. Payment shall be deemed received on the date funds are cleared and available in the Freelancer's designated account.

🇬🇧 UK Variant

For the avoidance of doubt, this Agreement constitutes a contract for the supply of goods or services for the purposes of the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (as amended), and the Freelancer reserves all rights and remedies under that Act.

✅ When to use

Every contract. This is your baseline payment clause. Even if you add milestone payments or deposits, you still need a clause defining the overall payment terms. Best for simple, one-deliverable projects.

Payment Clause 02

Milestone / Phase Payment Schedule

Milestone Payments. The total Fee of [AMOUNT] shall be payable in the following instalments, each due upon the Freelancer's completion of the corresponding milestone and submission of an invoice:

MilestoneDescriptionAmountDue Upon
1[Description][Amount]Completion & invoice
2[Description][Amount]Completion & invoice
3[Description][Amount]Completion & invoice

Each milestone payment shall be due within [NUMBER] calendar days of the relevant invoice date. The Freelancer shall not be obligated to commence work on any subsequent milestone until payment for the preceding milestone has been received in full. The Client shall have [NUMBER] business days from delivery to request reasonable revisions. Approval shall be deemed given if no written objection is received within that period.

✅ When to use

  • Projects worth more than £1,000
  • Projects lasting longer than 2–4 weeks
  • Any project with distinct phases
  • New clients you haven't built trust with yet

Payment Clause 03

Deposit / Retainer Clause

Deposit. The Client shall pay a non-refundable deposit of [AMOUNT] ([PERCENTAGE]% of the total Fee) (the "Deposit") prior to the commencement of any work. The Deposit shall be due within [NUMBER] calendar days of execution of this Agreement. The Freelancer shall not be obligated to commence the Services until the Deposit has been received in full and cleared funds are available. The Deposit shall be applied against the final invoice. The remaining balance of [AMOUNT] shall be invoiced upon completion and payable within [NUMBER] calendar days.

🇬🇧 UK Variant

The Deposit is non-refundable and represents a genuine pre-estimate of the Freelancer's costs in reserving capacity and declining other engagements. This clause shall not be construed as a penalty clause for the purposes of English law.

✅ When to use

Always recommended. Even a 25% deposit dramatically reduces your risk. 50% upfront / 50% on completion is the most common structure. Especially important for new clients, large projects, or international work.

Payment Clause 04

Late Payment Fee Clause

Late Payment. If the Client fails to make any payment due under this Agreement by the due date, the Freelancer shall be entitled, without prejudice to any other rights or remedies, to:

  1. charge interest on the overdue amount at the rate of 8% per annum above the Bank of England base rate, in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (as amended);
  2. claim fixed-sum compensation as prescribed by the said Act (£40 for debts up to £999.99; £70 for debts between £1,000 and £9,999.99; £100 for debts of £10,000 or more);
  3. recover all reasonable costs of collecting the overdue amount, including solicitor's fees, court fees, and debt recovery agency fees.

Interest shall accrue on a daily basis from the due date until the date of actual payment, whether before or after any judgment.

✅ When to use

Every contract. Even if you never enforce it, its presence discourages late payment. In the UK, the Late Payment Act gives you this right automatically — but having it in the contract makes enforcement easier and reminds the client what they're agreeing to.

Payment Clause 05

Kill Fee / Cancellation Clause

Cancellation and Kill Fee. Either party may terminate this Agreement by providing [NUMBER] days' written notice.

In the event of termination by the Client prior to completion:

  1. The Client shall pay for all work completed to date, calculated on a pro-rata basis or at [AMOUNT] per hour, whichever is greater;
  2. The Client shall pay a cancellation fee of [PERCENTAGE]% of the remaining balance (the "Kill Fee");
  3. Any Deposit paid shall be non-refundable.

Upon full payment, the Client receives a licence to use all completed deliverables. Incomplete work remains the Freelancer's property.

🇬🇧 UK Variant

This cancellation fee represents a genuine pre-estimate of the Freelancer's losses arising from early termination, including lost opportunity cost, rescheduling costs, and administrative burden. It is not intended as a penalty and shall be enforceable as a liquidated damages clause under English law.

✅ When to use

Every contract. Essential for large projects where you've blocked out weeks/months. A typical kill fee is 25–50% of the remaining balance.

Payment Clause 06

Scope Creep Protection Clause

Scope of Work and Change Orders. The Services are limited to those described in the Scope of Work. Any work outside the Scope constitutes a "Change Order."

No Change Order shall be commenced until: (a) the Freelancer has provided a written estimate of the additional cost and timeline impact; (b) the Client has approved in writing (email shall suffice); and (c) any required additional deposit has been received.

Change Orders shall be billed at [AMOUNT] per hour. This Agreement includes [NUMBER] rounds of revisions per deliverable. Additional revisions are Change Orders. The project timeline shall be extended proportionately for any approved Change Order.

✅ When to use

Every single contract. No exceptions. Scope creep is the #1 profit killer for freelancers. Define a specific number of revision rounds (typically 2–3). Attach a detailed Scope of Work — the more specific, the better.

Payment Clause 07

Rush Fee Clause

Expedited Timeline / Rush Fee. The standard delivery timeline is [NUMBER] business days from commencement. If the Client requests delivery in a shorter timeframe, the following surcharges apply:

Requested TimelineSurcharge
Less than 75% of standard[e.g. 25%] surcharge
Less than 50% of standard[e.g. 50%] surcharge
Less than 25% of standard[e.g. 100%] surcharge

Rush fees are subject to availability and acceptance. Where a rush fee is agreed and the Client subsequently causes delays, the rush fee remains payable in full.

✅ When to use

  • When your services have a standard turnaround time
  • Common in design, copywriting, translation, and development
  • If you regularly work with clients who have "urgent" requests

Payment Clause 08

Payment Method Specification

Payment Method. All payments shall be made via [PAYMENT METHOD] to the account details provided on the Freelancer's invoice.

All bank charges, transfer fees, and transaction costs shall be borne by the Client. The Freelancer shall receive the full invoiced amount without deduction.

The Client shall not make payment by any method other than those specified without prior written consent. Payments by cheque are not accepted unless explicitly agreed, and shall not be considered received until cleared.

The Client shall provide remittance advice or proof of payment to [EMAIL] within [NUMBER] business days of each payment.

✅ When to use

Every contract. Always specify how you want to be paid. Use bank transfer for larger amounts (lower fees). Use Wise for international payments. The proof-of-payment requirement prevents "but I already paid you" disputes.

Payment Clause 09

Currency and Exchange Rate Clause

Currency. All amounts in this Agreement are denominated in [CURRENCY CODE — e.g. GBP] (the "Contract Currency"). All invoices and payments shall be in the Contract Currency.

Exchange Rate Risk. If the Client pays from an account in a different currency, the Client bears all exchange rate risk and conversion fees. The Freelancer shall receive the full invoiced amount without shortfall.

Long-Term Adjustment. Where this Agreement exceeds [NUMBER] months and the exchange rate fluctuates by more than [PERCENTAGE]%, either party may request a good-faith renegotiation of the Fee.

Withholding Tax. The Client shall not withhold taxes from payments unless legally required. Where withholding is required, the Client shall gross up the payment so the Freelancer receives the full invoiced amount.

✅ When to use

  • Any contract with an international client
  • Contracts denominated in a foreign currency
  • Long-term contracts (3+ months) where rates could shift
  • Clients in jurisdictions that impose withholding taxes

Payment Clause 10

Dispute Resolution Clause

Dispute Resolution. In the event of any dispute arising from this Agreement, the parties shall:

  1. Negotiate — the aggrieved party shall give written notice and the parties shall use reasonable endeavours to resolve the dispute within [NUMBER] business days;
  2. Mediate — if unresolved, either party may refer the dispute to mediation administered by the Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution (CEDR). Costs shall be borne equally;
  3. Litigate — if mediation fails, either party may commence court proceedings. This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

Exception: The Freelancer may commence proceedings for the recovery of unpaid fees without first engaging in negotiation or mediation.

✅ When to use

Every contract. The escalation ladder (negotiate → mediate → litigate) saves time and money. The carve-out for unpaid fees is critical — don't agree to mandatory mediation for simple debt recovery.

Final Tips

Before You Use These Templates

  • Combine templates + clauses — Use a contract template from Part 1, then drop in payment clauses from Part 2 as needed
  • Number clauses sequentially — Renumber everything in your final contract for clarity
  • Attach a Scope of Work — The more specific your scope document, the better protected you are
  • Keep records — Save signed copies of all contracts, amendments, and correspondence
  • Review annually — Laws change. Update your templates at least once a year
  • Get professional advice — Use these as a starting point, then have a solicitor review your final contract at least once. The cost of a single legal review is far less than the cost of one bad contract
  • Send the NDA first — If you'll be discussing sensitive information, sign the NDA before the discovery call
  • Use Change Orders religiously — Every "small addition" gets a written Change Order. No exceptions.
