Xero vs FreeAgent 2026: Which Is Better for UK Freelancers?

Published 12 March 2026 · 9 min read

If you're a UK freelancer looking for accounting software in 2026, you've probably narrowed it down to two names: Xero and FreeAgent. Both are MTD-compatible, both are popular, and both will happily charge you £20-£40 a month for the privilege.

But which one is actually worth your money? And is there a chance you don't need either?

I've compared them across every category that matters to UK freelancers and sole traders. No affiliate links, no sponsored rankings — just an honest breakdown.

Quick Verdict

The Short Answer

FreeAgent wins for most solo freelancers. It's simpler, has built-in self-assessment filing, and is free with NatWest/RBS business accounts. Xero is better if you have employees, your accountant insists on it, or you need advanced features like multi-currency invoicing. If you earn under £50k, you might not need either — a well-structured spreadsheet could save you £200-£400/year.

Ease of Use

FreeAgent

FreeAgent was designed specifically for freelancers and sole traders. The interface assumes you're not an accountant. Bank feeds connect easily, expenses are straightforward, and the dashboard shows you your tax position at a glance. Most freelancers can be up and running within an hour.

Xero

Xero is more powerful but has a steeper learning curve. It was originally built for small businesses with accountants, and it shows. The reconciliation workflow is excellent once you learn it, but the sheer number of options can be overwhelming for someone who just wants to track income and expenses.

Winner: FreeAgent. If you're a solo freelancer without an accountant, FreeAgent is significantly easier to get started with.

FreeAgent: Pros and Cons

✅ Pros

  • Free with NatWest/RBS accounts
  • Built-in self-assessment filing
  • Real-time tax position forecast
  • Built-in mileage tracking
  • Designed specifically for freelancers
  • Simpler interface, less overwhelming
  • UK-focused (not a global tool adapted for UK)

❌ Cons

  • Limited multi-currency support
  • Fewer integrations than Xero
  • Less flexible reporting
  • Not ideal if you have an accountant who prefers Xero
  • Can feel basic as your business grows

Xero: Pros and Cons

✅ Pros

  • 1,000+ app integrations
  • Excellent multi-currency support
  • Strong invoicing and quoting
  • Most accountants know and prefer it
  • Scales well as your business grows
  • Better project costing and reporting

❌ Cons

  • No free tier (even with any bank)
  • Steeper learning curve
  • No built-in self-assessment filing
  • Starter plan is very limited (20 invoices/month)
  • Not specifically designed for UK freelancers
  • More expensive overall

When to Choose FreeAgent

  • You're a solo freelancer or sole trader
  • You bank with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle (free access)
  • You file your own self-assessment
  • You want the simplest possible setup
  • You drive for work and need mileage tracking
  • You don't need complex multi-currency features

When to Choose Xero

  • You have employees
  • Your accountant prefers or requires Xero
  • You invoice in multiple currencies regularly
  • You need extensive app integrations
  • You're planning to grow beyond sole trader
  • You need detailed project costing and reporting

The Budget Alternative Nobody Mentions

💡 Do You Actually Need Accounting Software?

Here's what nobody selling software wants you to hear: if you earn under £50,000/year, you might not need either Xero or FreeAgent.

The MTD threshold for 2026/27 is £50,000. Below that, you're not required to use MTD-compatible software. A well-structured spreadsheet can handle your bookkeeping perfectly well.

Even above the threshold, you have options. Bridging software (£50-£100/year) can submit your spreadsheet data to HMRC for MTD compliance — a fraction of what Xero or FreeAgent costs.

Annual cost comparison:

  • Xero Standard: £396/year
  • FreeAgent (paid): £228-£408/year
  • FreeAgent (NatWest/RBS): £0/year
  • Spreadsheet + bridging software: £50-£100/year
  • Spreadsheet only (under threshold): £0-£7

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Xero or FreeAgent better for UK freelancers?

FreeAgent is generally better for solo freelancers and sole traders. It's simpler, has built-in self-assessment filing, and is free with NatWest/RBS business accounts. Xero is better if you have employees, work with an accountant who prefers it, or need advanced multi-currency features.

How much do Xero and FreeAgent cost in 2026?

FreeAgent costs £19-£34/month (or free with NatWest/RBS business accounts). Xero costs £15-£47/month depending on the plan. Both are MTD-compatible.

Do I need accounting software for Making Tax Digital?

If you earn over £50,000 from self-employment or property, you'll need MTD-compatible software from April 2026. If you earn under the threshold, you can still use spreadsheets with bridging software, or budget tracking tools like our Tax Tracker spreadsheet.

Can I use a spreadsheet instead of Xero or FreeAgent?

Yes, if your income is under the MTD threshold (£50k in 2026/27). A well-structured spreadsheet with bridging software costs far less than monthly accounting subscriptions. Many freelancers earning under £50k find a spreadsheet template more cost-effective.

Is FreeAgent really free with NatWest?

Yes. NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, and Mettle business account holders get full FreeAgent access at no extra cost. It's a genuine freebie — the full product, not a stripped-down version.
